If every ring had the unique factorization property, life would be very boring indeed. And in fact, the failure of certain rings in algebraic number theory to have the unique factorization property played a role in several failed attempts to prove Fermat's Last Theorem, which says that there are no nontrivial integer solutions to the equation \(x^n + y^n = z^n\) if \(n \ge 3\text{.}\)
