Let \(R\) be a PID and suppose that a non-zero non-unit element \(a\in R\) can be factored in two different ways as a product of irreducibles.

\begin{equation*} a=p_1p_2\cdots p_r=q_1q_2\cdots q_s \end{equation*}

Assume that \(s\geq r\text{.}\) Since \(p_1\) is irreducible (and therefore prime), there exists \(j\) such that \(p_1 \mid q_j\text{.}\) Since \(q_j\) is irreducible as well, there is a unit \(u_1\) such that \(p_1=u_1 q_j\text{.}\) Up to reordering we have

\begin{equation*} p_1p_2\cdots p_r=u_1p_1q_2\cdots q_s \end{equation*}

Therefore

\begin{equation*} p_2\cdots p_r=u_1q_2\cdots q_s \end{equation*}

We can continue in a similar fashion until we end up with

\begin{equation*} 1=u_1\cdots u_rq_{r+1}\cdots q_s \end{equation*}

Since the \(q_j\)'s are non-units, this means that \(r=s\) and that the \(p_i\)'s are associates of the \(q_j\)'s. Therefore \(R\) is a UFD.
